Privacy

Last updated: 2026-05-26

The short version

Your collection stays on your devices. Kabynyt does not collect, transmit, or sell your data. There are no third-party trackers in the app.

What stays on-device

Every item you add — photos, notes, prices, metadata — is stored in SwiftData on your iPhone or iPad. If you have iCloud enabled, it syncs through your private CloudKit container to your other iOS devices. Apple cannot read this data; Kabynyt cannot read this data; nobody but you can read this data.

AI photo identification

When you ask Kabynyt to identify a collectible from a photo, the photo is sent to Google's Gemini 3.5 Flash model for analysis. Kabynyt does not see this traffic — the request goes from your device directly to Google with the Gemini API key bundled in the app (or your own key, if you supplied one in Settings). Google's privacy policy governs how Gemini processes the request. Photos are not retained by Kabynyt and are not used to train any model.

Barcode lookups

When you scan a barcode, the barcode value is sent to Google's Gemini 3.5 Flash model for a deep lookup that returns title, manufacturer, year, and market price range in one request. No identifying information about you or your device is sent — only the barcode digits.

Catalog enrichment

To fill in titles, covers, and metadata after a barcode scan or CSV import, Kabynyt queries public catalogs: the iTunes Search API (movies, TV, music, podcasts), Open Library (books), MusicBrainz and Cover Art Archive (music), Discogs (vinyl), and Wikipedia (general entries). Only the title, identifier, or barcode being looked up is sent — never your collection, your other items, or anything that identifies you.

This website

This website (kabynyt.com) is a static site, hosted on GitHub Pages and proxied through Cloudflare. It does not set cookies and loads no third-party scripts. Cloudflare and GitHub each maintain standard request logs (IP, user agent, page requested) per their own privacy policies; Cloudflare may also report aggregate page-load metrics through its built-in Web Analytics.
